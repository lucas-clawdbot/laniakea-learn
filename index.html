Before we dive into solutions, we need to deeply understand the problem. Laniakea isn't just "another DeFi upgrade" â€” it's a fundamental rethinking of how AI systems can safely manage institutional capital.

The Scale

Sky manages $9+ billion in stablecoin liabilities (USDS). This capital needs to be deployed into yield-generating opportunities to sustain the Sky Savings Rate (SSR) that sUSDS holders earn.

The core business: Sky borrows at SSR (what it pays sUSDS holders) and lends at higher rates across DeFi, TradFi, and RWAs. The spread is profit.

The Manual Problem

Currently, capital deployment requires:

  • Governance proposals (days to weeks)
  • Executive votes (more days)
  • Manual execution by trusted parties
  • Slow reaction to market changes

This worked at smaller scale. At $9B+, it becomes a bottleneck. Opportunities are missed. Reactions are slow. Coordination is expensive.

Why This Is Hard

1. Speed vs. Oversight

Markets move in milliseconds. Governance moves in days. If AI needs permission for every action, you lose the speed advantage. If it doesn't, you lose control.

Capital-deploying agents. The "investment funds" of Sky. They:

Wrappers around actual investments. The "SPVs" of Sky. They:

  • Manage capacity and redemptions

Types: Passthrough Halos (fungible, LCTS), Structuring Halos (bespoke, NFAT), Exchange Halos (trading), Core Halos (legacy assets).

4. Executors

Governance operators. They:

  • Execute privileged operations
  • Manage configuration changes
  • Coordinate between agents
