Let's start with the basics. Sky Ecosystem powers USDS — a large decentralized, yield-generating stablecoin. As of late 2025, we're talking about roughly ten billion dollars in USDS supply, over four hundred million in annualized protocol revenue, and about a hundred and sixty-eight million in annualized profit. This thing grew around eighty-six percent in 2025, outpacing the overall stablecoin market which grew about fifty percent. So it's not just big — it's growing faster than the category.  Sky evolved from MakerDAO, which launched back in 2017 with DAI. Eight years of continuous operation through multiple market cycles — the 2018 bear market, the March 2020 crash where ETH dropped over fifty percent in a single day, the 2022 crypto winter. That track record matters. DAI still works and is convertible one-to-one with USDS.  The revenue model is straightforward. Sky earns a base rate on deployed capital, pays a savings rate to sUSDS holders, and keeps the spread. Revenue comes from collateralized lending, digital asset-backed lending, U.S. Treasuries, private credit like CLO allocations, and delta-neutral strategies. Diversification means they're not dependent on any single market regime, which has historically dampened sensitivity to crypto price cycles.  Now let's talk tokens. There are three to know.  USDS is the stablecoin — pegged one-to-one to the dollar, overcollateralized by crypto assets, Treasuries, and credit positions, freely transferable, available on Ethereum mainnet plus multiple L2s and L1s via SkyLink. Unlike payment stablecoins that fall under frameworks like the GENIUS Act, USDS is specifically designed for capital formation and yield generation. There are about three hundred billion dollars in stablecoins sitting idle without earning yield right now. That's the market opportunity.  sUSDS is the savings token. It's an ERC-4626 vault. You deposit USDS, get sUSDS back, and the exchange rate increases continuously at the Sky Savings Rate. No lockups, freely redeemable at any time. It even earns yield on other chains through SkyLink. Simple and elegant.  SKY is the governance token. Fixed supply of 23.46 billion, emissions permanently disabled. The protocol buys back SKY on the open market using profits. Since February 2025, over ninety-two million dollars in buybacks — that's over six percent of total supply purchased. SKY holders get three things: governance participation over all protocol parameters, programmatic profit allocation through those buybacks, and collateral utility — meaning you can use staked SKY as collateral for borrowing USDS while still earning staking rewards. That last one is especially clever because you effectively lower your borrowing costs without sacrificing governance participation or yield.  Roughly seventy-five to a hundred percent of protocol profits flow to SKY stakers through the buyback mechanism. There's also a deflationary mechanic: about fourteen percent of the original MKR supply was never claimed after the migration, and the SKY backing that unclaimed MKR gets burned one percent per quarter over about twenty-four years.  Protocol revenue flows through a five-step waterfall called the Treasury Management Function. Step one takes care of security and maintenance — twenty-one percent during the genesis period, dropping to four to ten percent post-genesis. Step two fills the aggregate backstop capital — a solvency buffer targeting one-and-a-half percent of total supply, filling dynamically based on how far it is from target.
